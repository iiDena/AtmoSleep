Why relaxing sounds help you sleep


You turn around in your bed at night. You're trying to sleep but can't. You get recommended to listen to soft music or nature sounds. But does that work? If so, how?

Natural sounds make you feel relaxed because they're constant sounds of a satisfying tone. Your brain understands them as non-threatening noises, which helps decrease your fight-or-flight response. This helps lowering stress levels which gives you a more relaxed sleep. Traffic sounds or someone moving around in the apartment above you, may keep you awake. But nature sounds or other background sounds helps mask those sounds.

It's not only natural sounds that works on your brain. Sounds of engines, trains and other machines can work the same way. They're also non-threatening noises that are steady which means they mask other disturbing sounds. Music can to an extent. You would most likely like to listen to a slow song, with around 60 to 80 beats per minute. It might also help if the song that's being played is familiar to you, in that way the song will fade into the background easier.
