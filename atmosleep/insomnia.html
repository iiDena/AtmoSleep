INSOMNIA


Have you ever had trouble falling asleep, waking up during the night, waking up too early, not feeling fresh after a night's sleep, lack of focus? These are some of the symptoms of insomnia.

What is insomnia?


Firstly, Insomnia is a sleeping disorder that causes you to have trouble falling asleep. Insomnia can be short-term (acute) and long-term (chronic). It might come and go for some people. Studies have shown that around 30% of the world's population suffer from insomnia. Insomnia can be caused by stress from exams, losing a job, inconsistency in sleep or just your genes. Drugs and Mental health can cause insomnia too.

How to treat insomnia


Studies have shown that therapy known as CBTI (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) is the best way to treat insomnia. alcohol and THC, which is a component found in cannabis, are popular substances used to by people. However, THC and alcohol makes your insomnia worse, rather than better in the long term. Melatonin (sleeping pills) will only increase your sleep quality, or what we call your sleep efficiency, by a few percent. Long-term use of the pills can have negative effects on you.

The CBTI therapy helps change your habits, your view and focuses on stress associated with sleep which can vary from each person. To avoid getting in a situation where you might get insomnia it is important that you get quality sleep. You can read more about our 10 tips to improve your sleep here.
